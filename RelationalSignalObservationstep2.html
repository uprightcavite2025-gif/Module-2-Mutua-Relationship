Step 2 — Observe Interaction Changes
Page 1 of 2
During these days, pay attention to how conversations unfold.
Look for small indicators that participation may be changing.
Examples may include
  • someone continues explaining their idea instead of stopping early
  • someone raises a concern sooner than usual
  • someone adds additional context to the discussion
  • a conversation becomes more open instead of cautious
Research on psychological safety shows that when people repeatedly experience respectful and steady interaction signals, they become more willing to contribute ideas and concerns (Edmondson, 1999).
These small shifts are often the first signs that relational trust is strengthening.
1-Minute Conversation Scan
(Optional Practice Tool)
Page 2 of 2
During the next few days, you may use this short scan after a conversation or meeting.
It helps you quickly notice how your relational signal appeared during the interaction.

This is not required for every conversation.
Use it when you want to pause briefly and reflect on how the interaction unfolded.
You can write this on any medium you prefer — digital notes, phone notes, or a notebook.
After a conversation, ask yourself:
1️⃣ Did I apply my chosen relational signal?
Examples:
• Did I clarify before reacting?
• Did I invite concerns before closing the discussion?
• Did I allow the other person to finish speaking?
Yes / Partly / Not yet
2️⃣ What happened in the interaction?
Did the conversation become:
• more open
• neutral
• cautious
Trust research shows that interaction signals influence whether people feel comfortable contributing ideas and concerns (Edmondson, 1999).
3️⃣ What would I repeat next time?
Identify one small behavior you would keep or strengthen in your next interaction.
Example:
• Ask one more clarification question
• Pause before responding
• Invite input before concluding
Small adjustments repeated over time gradually shape interaction patterns.
Why this quick scan helps
Brief reflection immediately after an interaction helps reinforce behavioral learning.
Experiential learning research shows that short reflection moments strengthen the connection between action and awareness (Kolb, 1984).

Over time, this awareness helps behaviors become more intentional and consistent.
How you may use this tool
You may run this scan:
• after a meeting
• after a feedback conversation
• after a discussion where tension appeared
• after a decision conversation
You do not need to do it every time.
Even using it once or twice during the week can increase awareness of how relational signals appear in real conversations.
